# The Three Cycles

## 🌘 The Runner

Small, fast, jagged moon.

* Cycle: **9 days**
* Visibly tumbles slightly
* Irregular brightness
* Causes:

  * aggressive fauna
  * unstable tides
  * rapid fungal blooms
  * “thin sleep” among humans

People track it casually because it changes constantly.

---

## 🌕 The Elder

Massive slow moon.

* Cycle: **21 days**
* Dominates the night sky
* Bright enough for moon-shadows
* Causes:

  * stable weather bands
  * migration timing
  * crop resonance
  * ocean breathing patterns

Civilization tracks this one formally.
Calendars, taxes, wars, marriages.

---

## ☄️ The Visitor

The wandering catastrophe.

* Cycle: **every 63 Elder cycles**
* Roughly every **1323 days** (~3.6 years if Elder=21d)
* Not technically a moon
* Highly eccentric orbit
* Visible for weeks before conjunction

Its arrival perturbs *everything*:

* tides
* monster behavior
* magic
* births
* navigation
* maybe even your Cycle system itself

The Visitor is not a periodic event.
It is a civilization event.

Children remember where they were during a Visitor year.

---

# Key Conjunctions

## 🌘 Runner Black

Every 9 days

The Runner disappears entirely.

Effects:

* predators unusually bold
* scouting bonuses increase
* gathering nodes harder to detect
* thieves culturally active

Historically:
The Silent Raids of Korvel occurred during a triple Runner Black sequence. Entire grain districts vanished without alarms being raised.

---

## 🌕 Elder Full

Every 21 days

The world gets *bright*.

Effects:

* travel speed improves
* undead weaken
* sea routes stabilize
* social festivals

Historically:
The Accord of Glasswater was signed beneath an Elder Zenith because both armies refused to fight under “the Witnessing Light.”

---

## 🌘🌕 Split-Light

Every 63 days

Runner half-phase intersects Elder full.

This is the “silver-shadow” night.

Effects:

* double shadows
* optical distortions
* rare gathering materials appear
* beasts become territorial instead of aggressive

Players would LOVE this because it changes route logic instead of raw stats.

Historically:
The city of Naith discovered mirrored iron during Split-Light excavations after entire cliff faces sheared open overnight.

---

## 🌑 Twin Dark

Every 189 days

Both moons simultaneously dark.

The ocean becomes *wrong*.

Effects:

* tides become chaotic
* navigation penalties
* cave systems flood unpredictably
* strange subterranean creatures emerge

Historically:
The Drowning Winter began on a Twin Dark. Coastal maps became unusable for nearly a decade because shorelines permanently shifted.

---

# ☄️ Visitor Conjunctions

This is where history stops sounding like bookkeeping and starts sounding like prophecy.

---

## ☄️ The Red Visitor

Rare atmospheric alignment.

The Visitor appears crimson for weeks.

Effects:

* iron oxidation accelerates
* strange rust blooms
* blood-colored rain in some regions

Historical result:
The Bronze Orchard Collapse.

Entire agricultural empires lost tools and irrigation infrastructure to runaway corrosion over two seasons. Archaeologists still find forests of oxidized farming equipment fused into root systems.

---

## ☄️ The Tide Spear

Visitor + Elder periapsis.

Ocean tides become monstrous.

Effects:

* temporary land bridges
* exposed trench ruins
* inland flooding
* migratory leviathans

Historical result:
The Pilgrimage of Hollow Salt.

Thousands crossed exposed seabeds to colonize distant archipelagos before the tides returned and erased the routes forever.

---

## ☄️ The Long Night

Dust interaction dims both moons.

Can last for months.

Effects:

* crop failures
* fungal superspread
* nocturnal ecosystem explosion
* psychological instability

Historical result:
The Ash Choir period.

Mass synchronized dreams were recorded across multiple continents. Entire religions formed around shared nightmare imagery.

---

# Geological Weirdness 🌊🪨

THIS is where two moons + visitor becomes chef’s-kiss worldbuilding.

Earth geology assumes relative orbital stability.
Your world does not get that luxury.

So:

---

## Tidal Scarring

Coastlines look clawed.

Not smooth erosion.
Instead:

* stepped cliffs
* parallel bite-mark canyons
* giant tidal shelves

Because tides periodically overperform violently.

Some ancient ports are now halfway up mountainsides because the sea itself migrated.

---

## Resonance Fractures

Certain mountain ranges crack rhythmically.

The moons create repeating stress harmonics in tectonic zones.

This produces:

* unnaturally hexagonal valleys
* repeating cliff bands
* “singing caves”
* regions where stone naturally shears into plates

Perfect for fantasy architecture.

---

## Wandering Rivers

Riverbeds occasionally relocate after Visitor years.

Not gradually.
Catastrophically.

Entire civilizations learned to build “second cities” because eventually the river *will leave*.

---

## Breathing Forests

Some forests evolve around periodic mega-floods and moonlight cycles:

* root lattices above ground
* reflective leaves
* synchronized blooming
* floating seed mats

The ecosystem itself becomes cyclical infrastructure.

---

## The Glass Coasts

Visitor tidal heating occasionally liquefies silica-rich shorelines.

Result:

* black glass beaches
* fused cliff faces
* razor tidal fields

People harvest “stormglass” there during conjunction years.

---

This kind of world gives you a huge advantage mechanically:

The universe itself becomes content cadence.

You don’t need “random seasonal event #7.”
The sky is already the event scheduler. 🌌
